Classic New York–Style Bagels

Chewy, slightly crisp, and lightly malted — classic New York bagels with a traditional boil. Makes 8 bagels.

Yield: 8 bagels • Active time: ~1 hr 30 min • Total: ~2–3 hours

Ingredients

Dough

  • 4 cups (500 g) bread flour (high-gluten preferred)
  • 1½ tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp barley malt syrup (or honey)
  • 1½ tsp active dry yeast
  • 1¼ cups (300 ml) lukewarm water (≈100°F / 38°C)
  • 1 tbsp neutral oil (vegetable or canola)

Boiling Water Bath

  • 2 quarts (2 L) water
  • 2 tbsp barley malt syrup (or honey)
  • 1 tbsp baking soda (optional — for deeper crust color)

Optional Toppings

  • Sesame seeds, poppy seeds, everything seasoning, coarse salt

Equipment

  • Large mixing bowl or stand mixer with dough hook
  • Large pot for boiling
  • Parchment-lined baking sheet, slotted spoon, wire rack, kitchen scale (recommended)

Method

  1. Activate yeast
    Combine lukewarm water, yeast, and 1 tbsp barley malt syrup. Let sit 5 minutes until foamy.
  2. Make dough
    In a large bowl or stand mixer, mix bread flour and salt. Add yeast mixture and oil. Mix to form a shaggy dough. Knead by hand 10–12 minutes or with a dough hook 8–10 minutes until smooth, elastic, and slightly stiff (not sticky).
  3. First rise
    Shape into a ball, place in a lightly oiled bowl, cover, and let rise at room temperature 1–1½ hours, until doubled.
  4. Divide & shape
    Punch down, divide into 8 equal pieces (≈100–110 g each). Roll into tight balls. Poke a hole in the center of each with your finger and gently stretch to a 1.5–2" hole. Place on a floured, parchment-lined sheet. Cover and rest 15–20 minutes.
  5. Prepare water bath
    Bring 2 quarts water to a boil in a large pot. Add 2 tbsp barley malt syrup and baking soda if using. Reduce to a simmer.
  6. Boil bagels
    Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C). Working 2–3 at a time, boil bagels 1 minute per side. Use a slotted spoon to transfer back to the parchment. Add toppings immediately while wet.
  7. Bake
    Bake on middle rack 20–25 minutes, rotating once, until golden brown. Cool on a wire rack at least 10 minutes before serving.

Tips & Variations

  • Use high-gluten flour for maximum chewiness.
  • Barley malt syrup gives the classic flavor; honey is an accessible substitute.
  • For "everything" seasoning: mix 2 tbsp each sesame & poppy seeds, 2 tbsp dried onion flakes, 2 tbsp dried garlic flakes, and 1 tsp coarse salt.
  • To refresh day-old bagels: slice and toast or freeze individually wrapped for up to 3 months.

Enjoy with cream cheese, lox, butter, or your favorite spreads.
